# SaaSCoop Governance

## Purpose

This document describes the initial governance model of SaaSCoop as an open source cooperative initiative in structuring phase.

SaaSCoop aims to build digital and AI solutions through open collaboration, transparent decision-making and long-term community stewardship.

## Current status

SaaSCoop is not yet presented here as a formally incorporated cooperative.

At this stage, SaaSCoop is an initiative under organization, with the goal of validating its governance, legal structure, financial sustainability and community participation model.

## Participation roles

### Supporter

A supporter contributes financially, promotes the initiative or helps expand the community.

Supporters do not automatically become contributors, maintainers or cooperative members.

### Community member

A community member participates in discussions, suggests ideas and helps prioritize problems to be solved.

Community participation does not imply employment, ownership or formal cooperative membership.

### Contributor

A contributor helps with one or more of the following:

- code
- documentation
- testing
- design
- translation
- research
- educational content
- community organization

### Maintainer

A maintainer is responsible for reviewing contributions, organizing issues, maintaining repositories and protecting the integrity of the project.

Maintainers are expected to follow the Code of Conduct and act transparently.

### Future cooperative member

A future cooperative member is someone who may participate in the formal cooperative structure once SaaSCoop is legally established.

This role depends on future legal, accounting and governance validation.

## Decision-making

SaaSCoop aims to use transparent and participatory decision-making.

Whenever possible, decisions should be documented through:

- GitHub Issues
- GitHub Discussions
- Pull Requests
- meeting notes
- public roadmap updates

## Decision levels

### Operational decisions

Examples:

- documentation improvements
- issue triage
- minor repository changes
- small technical adjustments

These may be handled by maintainers.

### Project decisions

Examples:

- roadmap changes
- major technical architecture decisions
- creation or deprecation of repositories
- release priorities

These should be discussed publicly before approval.

### Strategic decisions

Examples:

- legal structure
- financial model
- fiscal sponsorship
- cooperative formalization
- remuneration principles
- partnerships

These require broader discussion and legal/accounting validation when applicable.

## Conflict of interest

SaaSCoop does not operate with the public sector.

Contributors and maintainers with public employment or other institutional obligations must avoid conflicts of interest and must not use privileged information, public resources or working hours from their employers.

## Financial transparency

SaaSCoop intends to adopt a transparent community funding model.

Financial resources, when collected, should be documented with clarity regarding:

- source of funds
- purpose
- expenses
- reserves
- project allocation
- decision process

## Remuneration principle

SaaSCoop aims to support dignified remuneration for contributors in the future, adjusted to local cost of living and project sustainability.

At the current stage, this principle is aspirational and depends on legal, financial and governance validation.

## Review cycle

This governance document should be reviewed periodically as the initiative matures.
